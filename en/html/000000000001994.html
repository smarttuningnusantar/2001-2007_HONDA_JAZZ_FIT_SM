DTC Troubleshooting: P0135 (41-2)
DTC P0135 (41-2):
 HO2S Heater Circuit Malfunction
  1. Reset the ECM/PCM.
  1. Start the engine.
Is DTC P0135 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose wires at the HO2S and at the ECM / PCM .■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the HO2S 4P connector.
  1. At the HO2S side, measure resistance between the HO2S 4P connector terminals No. 3 and No. 4.
Is there about 6 Ω (at room temperature)?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Replace the HO2S .■
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and the HO2S 4P connector terminals No. 3 and No. 4 individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Replace the HO2S .■
NO -
Go to Step 7 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between the HO2S 4P connector terminal No. 3 and No. 4.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Go to Step 12 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect ECM/PCM connector A (31P).
  1. Check for continuity between ECM/PCM connector terminal A1 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the ECM/PCM (A1) and the HO2S .■
NO -
Update the ECM/PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM/PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good ECM/PCM, replace the original ECM/PCM.■
  1. Measure voltage between the HO2S 4P connector terminal No. 3 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 13 .
NO -
Check the No. 16 (7.5A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box. If the fuse is OK, repair open in the wire between the HO2S and No. 16 (7.5A) fuse.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reconnect the HO2S 4P connector.
  1. Disconnect ECM/PCM connector A (31P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between ECM/PCM connector terminal A1 and A5.
Is there 0.1 V or less?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between the ECM/PCM (A1) and the HO2S .■
NO -
Update the ECM/PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM/PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good ECM/PCM, replace the original ECM/PCM.■
