Wheel Runout Inspection
  1. Raise the vehicle, and make sure it is securely supported.
  1. Check for a bent or deformed wheel.
  1. Set up the dial gauge as shown, and measure the axial runout by turning the wheel.
    Front and rear wheel axial runout:
    Standard:
    Aluminum wheel:
    0−0.7 mm (0−0.03 in.)
    Steel wheel:
    0−1.0 mm (0−0.04 in.)
    Service limit:
    2.0 mm (0.08 in.)

  1. Reset the dial gauge to the position shown, and measure the radial runout.
    Front and rear wheel radial runout:
    Standard:
    Aluminum wheel:
    0−0.7 mm (0−0.03 in.)
    Steel wheel:
    0−1.0 mm (0−0.04 in.)
    Service limit:
    1.5 mm (0.06 in.)

  1. If the wheel runout is not within the specification, check the wheel bearing end play, and make sure the mating surfaces on the brake disc or drum and the inside of the wheel are clean.
  1. If the bearing end play is within the specification but the wheel runout is more than the service limit, replace the wheel.
