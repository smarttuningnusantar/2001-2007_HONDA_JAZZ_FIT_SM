DTC Troubleshooting: P1882 (33-1)
DTC P1882:
 Problem in Inhibitor Solenoid Circuit
NOTE: Record all freeze data before you troubleshoot.
  1. Disconnect the inhibitor solenoid connector.
  1. Measure the resistance between connector terminals at the solenoid connector.
Is the resistance 17.1−21.0 Ω?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Replace the inertia switch.■
  1. Disconnect the battery negative terminal.
  1. Disconnect PCM connector C (22P).
  1. Check for continuity between PCM connector terminal C6 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to ground in the wire between PCM connector terminal C6 and the inertia switch.■
NO -
Go to Step 6 .
  1. Connect the inhibitor solenoid connector.
  1. Disconnect PCM connector A (31P).
  1. Measure the resistance between PCM connector terminals C6 and A4 or A5.
Is the resistance 17.1−21.0 Ω?
YES -
Check for loose terminal fit in the PCM connectors. If necessary, substitute a known-good PCM and recheck.■
NO -
Repair loose terminals or open in the wire between PCM connector terminals C6, A4 or A5 and the inertia switch.■
