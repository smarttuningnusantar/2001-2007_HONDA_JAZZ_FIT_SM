A/T Shift Cable Replacement
  1. Raise the vehicle, and make sure it is securely supported.
  1. Remove the center console.
  1. Shift into the N position.
  1. Remove the nut securing the shift cable end, then separate the shift cable end from the shift lever.
  1. Rotate the socket holder (A) on the shift cable (B) a quarter turn; the tab (C) on the socket holder will be in the opening of the shift lever bracket base (D). Then slide the holder to remove the shift cable from the shift lever bracket base.
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing and intake air duct.
  1. Remove the snap pin (A) and control pin (B) from the control lever (C).
  1. Remove the bolts securing the shift cable bracket plate (D), and remove the shift cable (E) from the shift cable bracket (F).
  1. Remove the shift cable bracket (A) and grommet (B), then pull the shift cable (C) out from the cabin.
  1. Insert the new shift cable through the grommet hole (D). Do not bend the shift cable excessively.
  1. Install the shift cable bracket on the body, then install the grommet.
  1. Apply molybdenum grease to the bore hole of a polyacetal resin (A) in the shift cable end (B), and attach the shift cable end to the control lever (C). Insert the control pin (D) through the control lever hole (E), through the shift cable end hole, and into the control lever slotted hole (F) in the direction shown. Push the control pin until its flange (G) contacts the control lever surface.
  1. Insert the snap pin (H) in the direction shown through the control pin hole and out the opening (I) of the control lever so that the hooked end (J) of the snap pin snaps into the countersunk hole (K) of the control pin.
  1. Secure the shift cable bracket plate (A) on the shift cable bracket (B) with the bolts.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and verify that the N position indicator comes on.
  1. If necessary, push the shift cable (A) until it stops, then release it. Pull the shift cable back two steps so that the transmission is in N. Do not push and pull the shift cable by holding the shift cable guide (B).
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Shift the shift lever into the N position.
  1. Insert a 6.0 mm (0.24 in.) pin (A) through the positioning holes (B) (C) on the shift lever bracket base and shift lever, and into the positioning hole (D) on the shift lever bracket base. The shift lever is secured in the N position.
  1. Align the socket holder (A) on the shift cable (B) with the slot in the bracket base (C), then slide the holder into base. Install the shift cable end (D) over the mounting stud (E) by aligning its square hole (F) with the square fitting (G) at the bottom of the stud. Rotate the holder a quarter turn to secure the shift cable. Do not install the shift cable by twisting the shift cable guide (H).
  1. Verify that the shift cable end is properly installed on the mounting stud.
  1. If improperly installed, remove the shift cable from the shift lever bracket base, and reinstall the shift cable. Do not install the shift cable end on the mounting stud while the shift cable is on the shift lever bracket case.
  1. Install and tighten the nut.
  1. Remove the 6.0 mm (0.24 in.) pin (A) that was installed to hold the shift lever.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and move the shift lever to each position. Verify that the A/T gear position indicator follows the transmission range switch.
  1. Start the engine, and check the shift cable adjustment with pressing the brake pedal.
  1. Push the shift lock release, and verify that the shift lever releases, and also check that the shift lever locks when it shifts back in the P position.
  1. Reinstall the center console.
  1. Install the air cleaner housing and air intake duct.
