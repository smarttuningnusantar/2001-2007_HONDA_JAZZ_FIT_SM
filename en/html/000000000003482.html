Subfuel Solenoid Replacement
  1. Relieve subfuel pressure.
  1. Remove the subfuel feed pipe bolts (A).
  1. Remove the intake manifold stay (A).
  1. Disconnect the connector (B) from the subfuel solenoid.
  1. Disconnect the No. 1, No. 2, No. 3 injector connectors, then remove the engine wire harness (C).
  1. Remove the subfuel pipe mounting bolts (D) from the subfuel pipe (E).
  1. Place the wrench (A), then remove the bolts (B).
  1. Remove the injector clip (C) from the subfuel solenoid.
  1. Coat the new O-ring (A) with clean engine oil, and insert the subfuel solenoid (B) into the subfuel solenoid holder (C).
  1. Install the injector clip (D).
  1. Coat the injector O-ring (E) with clean engine oil, and insert the subfuel solenoid into the subfuel pipe (F).
  1. Place the wrench (G), then tighten the bolts (H).
  1. Coat the fuel nozzle O-ring (A) with clean engine oil.
  1. Install the subfuel rail into the intake manifold (B).
  1. Install the subfuel rail mounting bolts (C).
  1. Connect the connector on the subfuel solenoid.
  1. Install the subfuel feed pipe bolts, and connect the quick-connect fittings.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), but do not operate the starter. After the subfuel pump runs for about 2 seconds, the subfuel pressure in the subfuel line rises. Repeat this two or three times, then check for fuel leakage.
  1. Install the intake manifold stay.
