  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II) and watch the malfunction indicator lamp (MIL).
Does the MIL come on and stay on?
YES -
If the MIL always come on and stays on, go to Step 81 . But if the MIL sometimes works normally, first check for these problems.
  • An intermittent short in the wire between the ECM/PCM (E29) and the data link connector (DLC) (or the service check connector).
  • An intermittent short in the wire between the ECM/PCM (E31) and the gauge assembly.
NO -
If the MIL is always off, go to Step 6 *1 (go to Step 13 ). But if the MIL sometimes works normally, first check for these problems.
  • A loose No. 3 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  • A loose No. 6 (40 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  • A loose No. 14 (15 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  • A loose No. 11 (15 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  • A poor connection at ECM/PCM terminal E31.
  • An intermittent open in the GRN/ORN wire between the ECM/PCM (E31) and the gauge assembly.
  • An intermittent short in the wire between the ECM/PCM (A21) and the manifold absolute pressure (MAP) sensor, CVT driven pulley speed sensor (CVT), CVT speed sensor (CVT).
  • An intermittent short in the wire between the ECM/PCM (A20) and the throttle position (TP) sensor, EGR valve position sensor, CVT drive pully speed sensor (CVT), idle mixture adjuster (IMA) (IN model).
  • An intermittent short in the wire between the ECM/PCM (E19) and the A/C temperature dail (without heater model).
    *1: With inertia switch
    *2: Without inertia switch
