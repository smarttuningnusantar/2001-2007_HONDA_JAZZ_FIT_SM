Fuel Pump Circuit Troubleshooting
If you suspect a problem with the fuel pump, check that the fuel pump actually runs; when it is ON, you will hear some noise if you listen to the fuel fill port with the fuel fill cap removed. The fuel pump should run for 2 seconds when the ignition switch is first turned on. If the fuel pump does not make noise, check as follows:
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Remove the fuse cover.
  1. Remove PGM-FI main relay 2 (A) from the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between PGM-FI main relay 2 4P connector terminal No. 3 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between PGM-FI main relay 1 and PGM-FI main relay 2.■
  1. Measure voltage between PGM-FI main relay 2 4P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Go to Step 7 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the inertia switch 3P connector.
  1. Connect inertia switch 3P connector terminals No. 1 and No. 3 with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between PGM-FI main relay 2 4P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Replace the inertia switch.■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the under-dash fuse/relay box and PGM-FI main relay 2.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Connect PGM-FI main relay 2 4P connector terminal No. 4 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Wait 10 seconds.
  1. Connect the ECM test harness and test pin box between the ECM and connectors.
  1. Check for continuity between test pin box terminal No. 90 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 17 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between PGM-FI main relay 2 and ECM connector No. 4 terminal No. 9 (test pin box terminal No. 90).■
  1. Reinstall PGM-FI main relay 2.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between pin box terminal No. 90 and body ground.

YES -
