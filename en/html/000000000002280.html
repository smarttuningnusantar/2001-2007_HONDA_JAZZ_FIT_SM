Engine Removal
Special Tools Required

Engine tilt hanger set

NOTE:
  • Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  • To avoid damage, unplug the wiring connectors carefully while holding the connector portion.
  • Mark all wiring and hoses to avoid misconnection. Also, be sure that they do not contact other wiring or hoses, or interfere with other parts.
  1. Secure the hood in the wide open position (support rod in the lower hole).
  1. Disconnect the battery negative cable first, then the positive cable from the battery.
  1. Remove the battery.
  1. Remove air cleaner housing.
  1. Remove the harness clamp (A), then remove the battery base (B).
  1. Disconnect the engine control module (ECM)/powertrain control module (PCM) connectors (A) and main wire harness connector (B).
  1. Remove the harness clamps (A) and grommet (B), then pull the engine wire harness through the bulkhead.
  1. Remove the battery cables (A) from the under-hood fuse/relay box, then remove the harness clamps (B).
  1. Disconnect the wire harness connector (C).
  1. Remove the throttle cable (A) by loosening the locknut (B), then slipping the cable end out of the accelerator linkage.
  1. Relieve fuel pressure:
    • Except KG, KE, KR models MT
    • KG, KE, KR models MT
    • CVT model
  1. Remove the evaporative emission (EVAP) canister hose (A) and brake booster vacuum hose (B).
  1. MT model: Remove the shift cable (A) and select cable (B). Take care not to bend the cables when removing them. Always replace any kinked cable with a new one.
  1. MT model: Remove the clutch slave cylinder (A) and clutch line bracket mounting bolt (B). Do not operate the clutch pedal once the slave cylinder has been removed.
  1. CVT model: Remove the snap pin (A) and control pin (B) from the control lever (C). Remove the mounting bolts (D) and harness clamp (E) from the shift cable bracket (F), then separate the shift cable (G) from the control lever. Do not bend the shift cable excessively.
  1. If the A/C compressor is equipped, remove the drive belt.
  1. Remove the radiator cap.
  1. Raise the hoist to full height.
  1. Remove the front wheels.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Loosen the drain plug in the radiator to drain the engine coolant.
  1. Drain the transmission fluid:
    • Manual transmission
    • CVT
  1. Drain the engine oil.
  1. Disconnect the secondary heated oxygen sensor (secondary HO2S) connector (A), then remove the three way catalytic converter (TWC) (B).
  1. Disconnect the stabilizer links.
  1. Disconnect the suspension lower arm ball joints.
  1. Remove the driveshafts. Coat all precision finished surfaces with clean engine oil. Tie plastic bags over the driveshaft ends.
  1. Remove the steering gearbox from the front subframe.
  1. Disconnect the compressor clutch connector (A), then remove the A/C compressor (B) without disconnecting the A/C hoses.
  1. Remove the condenser fan shroud.
  1. CVT model: Remove the automatic transmission fluid (ATF) cooler hoses, then plug the ATF cooler hoses and lines.
  1. Lower the hoist.
  1. Remove the upper radiator hose (A) and lower radiator hose (B).
  1. Remove the heater hoses.
  1. Attach the engine tilt hanger set to the engine as shown.
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), then remove the transmission mount bracket (B).
M/T:
CVT:
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), then remove the side engine mount/bracket assembly (B).
  1. Make sure the hoist brackets are positioned properly. Raise the hoist to full height.
M/T:
CVT:
  1. Remove the rear mount mounting bolts.
  1. MT model: Remove the left front mount/bracket assembly.
  1. Remove the front subframe.
  1. Check that the engine/transmission is completely free of vacuum hoses, fuel and coolant hoses, and electrical wiring.
  1. Slowly lower the engine about 150 mm (6 in.). Check once again that all hoses and wires are disconnected from the engine/transmission.
  1. Lower the engine all the way. Remove the chain hoist from the engine.
  1. Remove the engine from under the vehicle.
