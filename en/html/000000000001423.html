Driveshaft Dynamic Damper Replacement
Special Tools Required

Boot band pincers,
 Kent-Moore J-35910

 or equivalent commercially available
  1. Remove the inboard joint.
  1. Remove the dynamic damper band. Be careful not to damage the dynamic damper.
    If the dynamic damper band is an ear clamp type (A), lift up tab (B) with a screwdriver.
  1. Wrap the splines on the driveshaft with vinyl tape (A) to prevent damage to the dynamic damper.
  1. Remove the dynamic damper. Be careful not to damage the dynamic damper.
  1. Remove the vinyl tape.
  1. Wrap the splines with vinyl tape (A) to prevent damage to the dynamic damper.
  1. Install the dynamic damper onto the driveshaft, then remove the vinyl tape. Be careful not to damage the dynamic damper.
  1. Adjust the position of the new dynamic damper to the figure follow.
    Right driveshaft:
    516−520 mm (20.31−20.47 in.)

  1. Close the ear portion (A) of the band with a commercially available boot band pincers Kent-Moore J-35910 or equivalent (B).
  1. Check the clearance between the closed ear portion of the band. If the clearance is not within the standard, close the ear portion of the band farther.
  1. Install the inboard joint.
