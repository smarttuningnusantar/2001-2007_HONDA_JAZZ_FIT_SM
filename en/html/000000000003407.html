Engine Removal
Special Tools Required

Engine tilt hanger set
 07KAK-SJ40101

Engine hanger stay
 07KAK-SJ40200

Sub hanger stay
 07MAK-PY30100
NOTE:
  • Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  • To avoid damaging wire and terminals, unplug the wiring connectors carefully while holding the connector portion.
  • Mark all wiring and hoses to avoid misconnection. Also, be sure that they do not contact other wiring or hoses, or interfere with other parts.
  1. Secure the hood in the wide open position (support rod in the lower hole).
  1. Relieve the fuel pressure.
  1. Disconnect the battery negative cable first, then the positive cable.
  1. Remove the battery.
  1. Remove air cleaner housing.
  1. Remove the harness clamps (A), then remove the battery base (B).
  1. Remove the passenger's dashboard undercover, then disconnect the engine control module (ECM) connectors (A) and dashboard wire harness connector (B).
  1. Disconnect the accelerator pedal position (APP) sensor connector (A).
  1. Remove the harness clamps (B) and grommet (C), then pull the engine wire harness through the bulkhead.
  1. Remove the battery cables (A) from the under-hood fuse/relay box, then remove the harness clamp(s) (B).
  1. Disconnect the engine compartment wire harness connector (C).
  1. Remove the engine cover.
  1. Remove the Evaporative Emission (EVAP) canister hose (A) and brake booster vacuum hose (B).
  1. Remove the cable bracket (A), then disconnect the cable (B) from the top of the transmission housing. Take care not to bend the cables when removing them. Always replace any kinked cable with a new one.
  1. Remove the clutch slave cylinder (A) and clutch line bracket mounting bolt (B). Do not operate the clutch pedal once the slave cylinder has been removed.
  1. Remove the intake manifold bracket.
  1. Remove the quick-connect fitting cover (A), then disconnect the sub fuel feed hose.
  1. Remove the drive belt.
  1. Remove the radiator cap.
  1. Raise the vehicle on the hoist to full height.
  1. Remove the front wheels.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Loosen the drain plug in the radiator to drain the engine coolant.
  1. Drain the transmission fluid.
  1. Drain the engine oil.
  1. Disconnect the primary heated oxygen Sensor (primary HO2S) connector (A).
  1. Disconnect the secondary heated oxygen Sensor (secondary HO2S) connector (B).
  1. Remove the three way catalytic converter (TWC)(C).
  1. Disconnect the stabilizer links.
  1. Disconnect the suspension lower arm ball joints.
  1. Remove the driveshafts. Coat all precision finished surfaces with clean engine oil. Tie plastic bags over the driveshaft ends.
  1. Remove the steering gearbox from the front subframe.
  1. Disconnect the A/C compressor clutch connector (A), then remove the A/C compressor (B) without disconnecting the A/C hoses.
  1. If the A/C compressor is equipped, remove the A/C condenser fan shroud.
  1. Lower the vehicle on the hoist.
  1. Remove the upper radiator hose (A) and lower radiator hose (B).
  1. Remove the heater hoses.
  1. Install the engine hanger plate.
  1. Attach the engine tilt hanger set to the engine as shown.
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), then remove the transmission mount bracket (B).
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), then remove the side engine mount/bracket assembly (B).
  1. Make sure the hoist brackets are positioned properly. Raise the vehicle on the hoist to full height.
  1. Remove the rear mount mounting bolts.
  1. Remove the left front transmission mount/bracket assembly.
  1. Remove the two clips (A) securing the under covers, then remove the front sub-frame (B).
  1. Check that the engine/transmission is completely free of vacuum hoses, fuel and coolant hoses, and electrical wiring.
  1. Slowly lower the engine/transmission about 150 mm (6 in.). Check once again that all hoses and wires are disconnected from the engine/transmission, then lower the engine all the way.
  1. Remove the chain hoist from the engine/transmission.
  1. Raise the vehicle all the way.
    NOTE: Carefully position the A/C compressor and steering gearbox.
  1. Remove the engine/transmission from under the vehicle.
