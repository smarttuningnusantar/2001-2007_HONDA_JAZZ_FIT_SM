DTC Troubleshooting: P0722 (35-3)
DTC P0722:
 Problem in CVT Driven Pulley Speed Sensor Circuit
For KB, KE, KG, and KQ Models
NOTE:
  • Record all freeze data and review General Troubleshooting Information before you troubleshoot.
  • The illustration shows the example of connector terminal arrangement; connector terminal arrangement varies with the models.
  1. Check whether the DTC for manifold absolute pressure (MAP) sensor is indicated.
Is the DTC for MAP sensor indicated?
YES -
Perform the MAP sensor troubleshooting, and recheck for DTC P0722 after troubleshooting.
NO -
Go to Step 2 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check for continuity between PCM connector terminals A23 and body ground, and between A24 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminals A23 and A24, and ground (G101), or repair poor ground (G101).■
  1. Disconnect CVT driven pulley speed sensor connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between CVT driven pulley speed sensor connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Go to Step 19 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect PCM connector C (22P).
  1. Check for continuity between CVT driven pulley speed sensor connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between PCM connector terminal C15 and the CVT driven pulley speed sensor connector.■
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Check for continuity between CVT driven pulley speed sensor connector terminal No. 3 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between CVT driven pulley speed sensor connector terminal No. 3 and ground (G101), or repair poor ground (G101).■
  1. Connect PCM connector C (22P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between CVT driven pulley speed sensor connector terminals No. 2 and No. 3.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 14 .
NO -
Go to Step 23 .
  1. Connect CVT driven pulley speed sensor connector.
  1. Measure the voltage between PCM connector terminals C15 and A23 or A24.
Is there 0 V or about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 16 .
NO -
Replace the CVT driven pulley speed sensor. ■
  1. Shift to the P position.
  1. Start the engine, and let it idle.
  1. Shift to the D position, and measure the voltage between PCM connector terminals C15 and A23 or A24 with engine idling.
Is there 1.5−3.5 V?
YES -
Update the PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good PCM, replace the original PCM .■
NO -
Replace the CVT driven pulley speed sensor. ■
  1. Measure the voltage between PCM connector terminals A21 and A23 or A24.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminal A21 and CVT driven pulley speed sensor connector.■
NO -
Go to Step 20 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect PCM connector A (31P).
  1. Check for continuity between PCM connector terminal A21 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
