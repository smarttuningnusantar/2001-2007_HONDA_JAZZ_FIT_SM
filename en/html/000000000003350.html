EPS Indicator Circuit Troubleshooting
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), start the engine and watch the EPS indicator.
Does the EPS indicator come on?
YES -
If the EPS indicator comes on and goes off, it's OK. If the EPS indicator stays on blinks, alway check for an open or a short to ground in the SCS circuit.
NO -
Go to Step 2 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, then ON (II) again and watch the brake system indicator.
Does the brake system indicator come on?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Repair an open in the indicator power source circuit.■
  • Blown No. 16 METER (7.5 A) fuse.
  • Open in the wire between the No. 16 METER (7.5 A) fuse and the gauge assembly.
  • Open circuit inside the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  • Faulty the gauge assembly.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the gauge assembly connector A (20P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the gauge assembly connector A (20P) terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Repair an open in the wire between the gauge assembly and No. 16 METER (7.5 A) fuse.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reconnect the gauge assembly connector A (20P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Reconnect the gauge assembly connector C (40P) terminal No. 27 to body ground with a jumper wire.
Does EPS indicator come on?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Faulty the gauge assembly.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery.
  1. Disconnect the EPS control unit connector B (16P).
  1. Reconnect the negative cable to the battery.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
With day time meter:
Without day time meter:
  1. Connect the EPS control unit connector B (16P) terminal No. 15 to body ground with a jumper wire.
Does EPS indicator come on?
YES -
Go to Step 17 .
NO -
Repair an open in the wire between the gauge assembly and EPS control unit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery.
  1. Disconnect the EPS control unit connector A (7P).
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and the EPS control unit connector A (7P) terminal No. 4 to body ground.
Is there continuty?
YES -
Go to Step 21 .
NO -
Repair an open in the wire between the EPS control unit and body ground (G451).■
  1. Reconnect the negative cable to the battery.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the EPS control unit connector B (16P) terminal No. 8 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 24 .
NO -
Repair an open in the wire between the EPS control unit connector B (16P) and No. 16 METER (7.5 A) fuse.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reconnect the EPS control unit connectors A and B.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
Does EPS indicator come on?
YES -
The system is OK at this time.
NO -
Check for loose EPS control unit connectors. If necessary, substitute a known-good EPS control unit and recheck.■
