DTC Troubleshooting: P0113 (10-2)
DTC P0113 (10-2):
 IAT Sensor Circuit High Voltage
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Check the IAT with the HDS or the scan tool.
Is −20°C (−4°F) or less, or about 5 V indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Intermitent failure, system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the IAT sensor and at the ECM/PCM.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the IAT sensor 2P connector.
  1. Connect the IAT sensor 2P connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2 with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Check the IAT with the HDS or the scan tool.
Is −20°C (−4°F) or less, or about 5 V indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Replace the IAT sensor .■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Remove the jumper wire.
  1. Connect ECM/PCM connector terminals A10 and B17 with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Check the IAT with the HDS or the scan tool.
Is −20°C (−4°F) or less, or about 5 V indicated?
YES -
Update the ECM/PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM/PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good ECM/PCM, replace the original ECM / PCM .■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the ECM/PCM (A10, B17) and the IAT sensor.■
