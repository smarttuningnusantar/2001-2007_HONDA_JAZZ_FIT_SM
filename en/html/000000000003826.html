How to Check for DTCs with the Special Tool to Short-circuit SCS Signal Circuit
Special Tool

  1. When the [D] indicator (A) in the gauge assembly (B) is blinking, check whether the Malfunction Indicator Lamp (MIL) comes on.
  1. If the [D] indicator blinks and MIL comes on at the same time, first check the fuel and emissions system as indicated by the DTC (except for DTC 70, DTC 70 means there is one or more A/T DTCs, and no problems were detected in the fuel and emissions system circuit of the PCM.)
  1. When the [D] indicator has been reported on, connect the special tool to the DLC (A) located under the dash behind the center console.
  1. Connect the jumper wire (B) between the special tool terminals 4 and 9, then turn the the switch (C) on.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and observe the [D] indicator for DTCs.Code 1 through 9 are indicated by individual short blinks. Code 10 and above are indicated by a series of long and short blinks. One long blink equals 10 short blinks. Add the long and short blinks together to determine the code.
  1. Record all A/T DTCs.
  1. Inspect the system according to the DTC troubleshooting.
  1. After the troubleshooting, reset the PCM memory by removing the No. 6 ECU fuse (15A) in the under-dash fuse relay box for more than 10 seconds.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, and disconnect the special tool from the DLC.
