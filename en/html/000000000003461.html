Breather Line and Suction Line Inspection
  1. Remove the access panel from the floor.
  1. Disconnect the suction hose (A) form the fuel pump, and connect a vacuum pump (B).
  1. Start the engine, and check the vacuum.
Is there vacuum?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Replace the fuel filter. ■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Connect a vacuum pump (A) to the suction hose, and apply vacuum to the hose (B).
Does the hold vacuum?
YES -
  • Check the blockage on the suction line.
  • Replace the 3-way joint.■
NO -
Go to Step 6 .
  1. Disconnect the breather hose (A) of the filler side from the 3-way joint (B), and set the rubber cap (C) to the 3-way joint.
  1. Connect a vacuum pump (A) to the suction hose, and apply vacuum to the hose (B).
Does the hold vacuum?
YES -
Check the blockage between the 3-way joint and the fuel tank vapor liquid separation valve on the breather line. If line is OK, replace the fuel tank. ■
NO -
Go to Step 8 .
  1. Connect a vacuum pump (A) to the breather hose (B) of the filler side, and apply air pressure to the hose.
Does the hold vacuum?
YES -
Check the blockage between the 3-way joint and the filler neck on the breather line.■
NO -
Go to Step 9 .
  1. Remove the rubber cap from the 3-way joint, and reconnect the breather hose to the 3-way joint.
  1. Connect a vacuum pump (A) to the suction hose (B), and apply air pressure to the hose.
Does the hold air pressure?
YES -
The breather line and the suction line are OK.■
NO -
  • Check the vacuum leak on the suction hose.
  • Replace the 3-way joint.■
