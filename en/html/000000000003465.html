Fuel Nozzle Replacement
  1. Relieve subfuel pressure.
  1. Remove the subfuel feed pipe bolts (A).
  1. Remove the intake manifold stay (A).
  1. Disconnect the connector (B) from the subfuel solenoid.
  1. Disconnect the No. 1, No. 2, No. 3 injector connectors, then remove the engine wire harness (C).
  1. Remove the subfuel pipe mounting bolts (D) from the subfuel rail (E).
  1. Remove the clip (F), and the fuel nozzle (G).
  1. Coat the new O-ring (black) (A) with clean engine oil, and insert the fuel nozzle (B) with the clip (C) into the subfuel pipe (D).
  1. Coat the new O-ring (brown) (E) with clean engine oil, insert the fuel nozzle. Install the subfuel rail into the intake manifold (F).
  1. Install the subfuel rail mounting bolts (G).
  1. Connect the connectors on the subfuel solenoid.
  1. Install the subfuel feed pipe bolts, and connect the quick-connect fittings.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), but do not operate the starter. After the subfuel pump runs for about 2 seconds, the fuel pressure in the fuel line rises. Repeat this two or three times, then check for fuel leakage.
  1. Install the intake manifold stay.
