DTC Troubleshooting: P1706 (6-1)
DTC P1706:
 Open in Transmission Range Switch Circuit
NOTE: Record all freeze data before you troubleshoot.
  1. Check whether the DTC P1705 is indicated.
Is the DTC P1705 indicated?
YES -
Perform the DTC P1705 troubleshooting, then go to step 2 after troubleshooting.
NO -
Go to Step 8 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reset the PCM memory by removing the No. 20 ECU (15A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box for more than 10 seconds.
  1. Start off in [D] position, and drive the vehicle until vehicle speed reaches 60 km/h (37 mph), then slow down and stop.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, then turn it ON (II) again.
  1. Start off in [D] position, and drive the vehicle until vehicle speed reaches 60 km/h (37 mph), then slow down and stop.
  1. While remaining the ignition switch ON (II) position, recheck for DTC P1706?
Is the DTC P1706 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
The problem has been corrected.■
  1. Test the transmission range switch.
Is the switch OK?
YES -
Connect the transmission range switch connector, then go to Step 9 .
NO -
Replace the transmission range switch .■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check for continuity between PCM connector terminal A23 and body ground, and between terminal A24 and body ground.
Are there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire PCM connector terminals A23 and A24, and ground (G101), or repair poor ground (G101).■
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Shift to [D] position.
  1. Measure the voltage between PCM connector terminals C20 and A23 or A24.
Is there voltage?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminal C20 and the transmission range switch .■
NO -
Go to Step 14 .
  1. Shift to [R] position.
  1. Measure the voltage between PCM connector terminal C10 and A23 or A24.
Is there voltage?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminal C10 and the transmission range switch .■
NO -
Go to Step 16 .
  1. Shift to [P] or [N] position.
  1. Measure the voltage between PCM connector terminals C12 and A23 or A24.
Is there voltage?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminal C12 and the transmission range switch .■
NO -
Go to Step 18 .
  1. Shift to [S] position.
  1. Measure the voltage between PCM connector terminals C9 and A23 or A24.
Is there voltage?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminal C9 and the transmission range switch .■
NO -
Go to Step 20 .
  1. Shift to [L] position.
  1. Measure the voltage between PCM connector terminals C11 and A23 or A24.
Is there voltage?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminal C11 and the transmission range switch .■
NO -
Update the PCM if it does not have the latest software, or subsutitute a known-good PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/ indication goes away with a known-good PCM , replace the original PCM.■
