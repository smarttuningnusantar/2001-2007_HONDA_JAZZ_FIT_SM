DTC Troubleshooting: 10
DTC 10:
 IAT Sensor Circuit Malfunction
  1. Reset the ECM/PCM.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
Is the MIL on and does it indicate DTC 10?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the IAT sensor and at the ECM / PCM .■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the IAT sensor 2P connector.
  1. Measure resistance between the IAT sensor 2P connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2.
Is there 0.4−4.0 kΩ?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Replace the IAT sensor .■
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. At the engine wire harness side, measure voltage between the IAT sensor 2P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Go to Step 9 .
  1. Measure voltage between the IAT sensor 2P connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Update the ECM/PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM/PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good ECM/PCM, replace the original ECM/PCM.■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the ECM / PCM (A10) and the IAT sensor .■
  1. Measure voltage between ECM/PCM connector terminals A10 and B17.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between the ECM / PCM (B17) and the IAT sensor .■
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect ECM/PCM connector B (24P).
  1. Check for continuity between ECM/PCM connector terminal B17 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the ECM / PCM (B17) and the IAT sensor .■
NO -
Update the ECM/PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM/PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good ECM/PCM, replace the original ECM/PCM.■
