DTC Troubleshooting: P102B (120)
DTC P102B:
 Subfuel solenoid Circuit Malfunction
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Select SUBFUEL SOLENOID ON in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P102B indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 26 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the subfuel solenoid, the subfuel pump relay, and the ECM.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check the SUBFUEL PUMP (15 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Go to Step 8 .
  1. Disconnect the SUBFUEL PUMP (15 A) fuse from the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  1. Disconnect the subfuel solenoid 2P connector.
  1. Remove the subfuel pump relay from the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  1. Check for continuity between subfuel solenoid 2P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the SUBFUEL PUMP (15 A) fuse, the subfuel pump relay, and the subfuel solenoid, then go to Step 29 .
NO -
Repair short in the wire between the subfuel pump relay and the subfuel pump, then go to Step 29 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the subfuel solenoid 2P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between subfuel solenoid 2P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 16 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the SUBFUEL PUMP (15 A) fuse and the subfuel solenoid, then go to Step 28 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. At the subfuel solenoid side, measure resistance between subfuel solenoid 2P connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2.
Is there about 9.78−10.82 Ω at room temperature?
YES -
Go to Step 18 .
NO -
Go to Step 27 .
  1. At the subfuel solenoid side, check for continuity between body ground and subfuel solenoid 2P connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2 individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 27 .
NO -
Go to Step 19 .
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 5 (9P).
  1. Check for continuity between subfuel solenoid 2P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the ECM connector No. 5 terminal No. 6 (test pin box terminal No. 127) and the subfuel solenoid, then go to Step 29 .
NO -
Go to Step 21 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between subfuel solenoid 2P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Repair short to power in the wire between the ECM connector No. 5 terminal No. 6 (test pin box terminal No. 127) and the subfuel solenoid, then go to Step 28 .
NO -
Go to Step 23 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, and wait 10 seconds.
  1. Connect the ECM test harness and the test pin box to the wire harness only, not to the ECM,.
  1. Connect subfuel solenoid 2P connector terminal No. 2 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between test pin box terminal No. 127 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 36 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the ECM connector No. 5 terminal No. 6 (test pin box terminal No. 127) and the subfuel solenoid, then go to Step 29 .
  1. Replace the subfuel solenoid.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Do the ECM RESET in the CLEAR MENU with the HDS.
  1. Do the ETCS learn procedure.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Select SUBFUEL SOLENOID ON in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P102B is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the subfuel solenoid, the subfuel pump relay, and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC troubleshooting.
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete.■
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Select subfuel solenoid ON in the INSPECTION MENU with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P102B is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the subfuel solenoid, the subfuel pump relay, and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC troubleshooting.
NO -
Replace the original ECM. ■
