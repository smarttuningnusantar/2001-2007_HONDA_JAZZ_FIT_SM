DTC Troubleshooting: P0141 (65)
DTC P0141:
 Secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) Heater Circuit Malfunction
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Do the DTC CLEAR in the CLEAR MENU with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Is DTC P0141 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, system is OK at this time.Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) and the ECM.■
  1. Check the No. 16 (7.5A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Repair short in the wire between the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) and the No. 16 (7.5A) fuse, then Go to Step 23 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) 4P connector.
  1. At the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) side, measure resistance between secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) 4P connector terminals No. 3 and No. 4.
Is there about 8.1−9.9Ω at room temperature?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Go to Step 20 .
  1. At the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) side, check for continuity between body ground and secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) 4P connector terminals No. 3 and No. 4 individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 20 .
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure voltage between secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) 4P connector terminals No. 3 and No. 4.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Go to Step 15 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, and wait 10 seconds.
  1. Connect the ECM test harness and the test pin box to the wire harness only, not to the ECM.
  1. Check for continuity between test pin box terminal No. 125 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the ECM connector No. 5 terminal No. 4 (test pin box terminal No. 125) and the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2), then go to Step 22 .
NO -
Go to Step 28 .
  1. Measure voltage between secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) 4P connector terminal No. 4 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 16 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) and the No. 16 (7.5A) fuse, then go to Step 22 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, and wait 10 seconds.
  1. Reconnect the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) 4P connector.
  1. Connect the ECM test harness and the test pin box to the wire harness only, not to the ECM.
  1. Measure voltage between test pin box terminals No. 125 and No. 4.
Is there 0.1V or less?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between the ECM connector No. 5 terminal No. 4 (test pin box terminal No. 125) and the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2), then go to Step 22 .
NO -
Go to Step 28 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Replace the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2).
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Do the ECM RESET in the CLEAR MENU with the HDS.
  1. Do the ETCS learn procedure.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P0141 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC troubleshooting.
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete.■
  1. Substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P0141 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the secondary HO2S (Sensor 2) and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC troubleshooting.
NO -
Replace the original ECM. ■
