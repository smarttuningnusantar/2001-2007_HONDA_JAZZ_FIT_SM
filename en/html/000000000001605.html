DTC Troubleshooting: 22, 23
DTC 22:
 Vehicle Speed Sensor Signal
DTC 23:
 Engine Speed Signal
NOTE:
  • Information marked with an asterisk ( * ) applies to CVT model.
  • If the MIL indicator is ON, troubleshoot the PGM-FI system first.
  • When the engine is running at 2,500 rpm or above and the vehicle speed is 0 km/h (0 mph) or below for 30 seconds (with added), the EPS indicator comes on.
  • When the vehicle speed is 10 km/h (6 mph) or above and the engine is running at 280 rpm (min −1 ) or below for 3 seconds, the EPS indicator comes on.
  1. Start the engine and check the tachometer.
Is the tachometer working correctly?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Go to Step 12 .
  1. Test-drive the vehicle above 3 mph (5 km/h).
Is the speedometer working correctly?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Perform the speedometer system troubleshooting. ■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery.
  1. Disconnect the vehicle speed sensor (VSS) 3P connector (ECM/PCM connector E (31P) ) * and EPS control unit connector B (14P).
  1. Check for continuity between the EPS control unit connector B (14P) terminal No. 4 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between the VSS (ECM/PCM) * and EPS control unit.■
NO -
Go to Step 7 .
  1. Connect the VSS 3P connector (ECM/PCM connector E (31P)) * .
  1. Block the rear wheels and raise the vehicle, and support it with safety stands in the proper locations.
  1. Reconnect the negative cable to the battery.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Block the right front wheel, and slowly rotate the left front wheel, and measure the voltage between the EPS control unit connector B (14P) terminal No. 4 and body ground.
Does the voltage pulse 0 V and 5 V?
YES -
Check for loose EPS control unit connectors.If necessary, substitute a known-good EPS control unit and recheck.■
NO -
Repair open or short to body ground in the wire between the EPS control unit and VSS (ECM/PCM) * or faulty the VSS.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery.
  1. Disconnect the EPS control unit connector B (14P).
  1. Reconnect the negative cable to the battery.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Measure the voltage between the EPS control unit connector B (14P) terminal No. 14 and body ground.
Is there about 0 V to battery voltage?
YES -

  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.

  1. Check for continuity between the EPS control unit connector B (14P) terminal No. 14 and body ground.
