DTC Troubleshooting: N
DTC indicator N:
 A Problem in the Blower Motor Circuit
  1. Clear the DTC by turning the ignition switch OFF, and then ON (II).
  1. Operate the climate control system in several modes.
  1. Check for DTCs using the self-diagnostic.
Is DTC N indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, check for loose wires or poor connections on the blower motor circuit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check the No. 5 (40A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box, and the No. 10 (7.5A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
Are the fuses OK?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Replace the fuse(s), and recheck.■
  1. Connect the No. 2 terminal of the blower motor 2P connector to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
Does the blower motor run?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Go to Step 23 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Disconnect the power transistor 4P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 2 terminal of the power transistor 4P connector and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Check for an open in the BLK wire between the power transistor and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G402.■
  1. Connect the No. 2 and No. 4 terminals of the power transistor 4P connector with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
Does the blower motor run at high speed?
YES -
Go to Step 14 .
NO -
Repair open in the BLU/BLK wire between the power transistor and the blower motor.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Disconnect climate control unit connector.
  1. Check for continuity between the No. 13 and No. 14 terminals of climate control unit 28P connector and body ground individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair any short to body ground in the wire(s) between the climate control unit and the power transistor.■
NO -
Go to Step 18 .
  1. Check for continuity between the following terminals of climate control unit 28P connector and power transistor 4P connector.
    28P:
    4P:
    No. 13
    No. 1

YES -
Go to Step 19 .
NO -
Repair any open in the wire(s) between the climate control unit and the power transistor.■
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Check for voltage between the No. 13 and No. 14 terminals of climate control unit 28P connector and body ground individually.
Is there 1 V or more?
YES -
Repair short to power in the wires.■
NO -
Go to Step 21 .
  1. Reconnect climate control unit 28P connector.
  1. Test the power transistor.
Is the power transistor OK?
YES -
Check for loose wire or poor connections at climate control unit 28P connector and at the power transistor 4P connector. If the connections are good, substitute a known-good climate control unit, and recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original climate control unit .■
NO -
Replace the power transistor.■
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Disconnect the blower motor 2P connector.
  1. Measure the voltage between the No. 1 terminal of the blower motor 2P connector and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -

NO -
