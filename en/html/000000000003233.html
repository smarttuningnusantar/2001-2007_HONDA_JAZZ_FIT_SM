Vehicle Speed Signal Circuit Troubleshooting
Special Tools Required:

Test Harness
 07LAJ-PT30200
M/T
Before testing, inspect the No. 3 (7.5A) fuses in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  1. Disconnect the 3P connector from the vehicle speed sensor (VSS) (A).
  1. Connect the test harness only to the engine wire harness.
  1. Connect the RED test harness clip (B) to the positive prove of an ohmmeter. Cover the white (C) and green (D) test harness with protective tape (E).
  1. Check for continuity between the RED test harness clip and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Repair open in the BLK wire between the VSS and G101.■
  1. Connect the WHT test harness clip (B) to the positive probe of a voltmeter, and connect the RED test harness clip (C) to the negative probe.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Repair open in the BLK/YEL wire between the VSS and the under-dash fuse/relay box.■
  1. Disconnect the WHT test harness clip (B).
  1. Connect the GRN test harness clip (D) to the positive probe of a voltmeter.
Is there 5 V or more?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Repair short in the BLU/WHT wire between the VSS and the cruise control unit, or the ECM .■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Connect the other test harness connector (A) to the VSS (B).
  1. Raise the front of the vehicle, and make sure it is securely supported.
  1. Put the vehicle in neutral with the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Slowly rotate one wheel with the other wheel blocked.
Does voltage pulse from 0 to about 5 V or more?
YES -
Go to Step 14 .
NO -
Replace the VSS .■
  1. Disconnect the 40P connector ‘‘C'' from the gauge assembly.
  1. Touch a probe to the BLU/YEL [BLU/WHT] wire, and connect it to body ground through a voltmeter.
  1. Slowly rotate one wheel with the other wheel blocked.
Does the voltmeter indicate pulsing voltage?
YES -
Replace the speedometer assembly.■
NO -
Repair open in the BLU/YEL [BLU/WHT] wire between the VSS and the speedometer.■
CVT
Before testing:
  • Check to see if diagnostic trouble code P1607 is indicated. If it is, troubleshoot the DTC.
  • Inspect the No. 3 (7.5A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box.
  • Check for continuity between the gauge assembly 40P connector C15 terminals and body ground (G401).
  1. Raise the vehicle, and make sure it is securely supported.
  1. Disconnect the 40P connector ‘‘C'' from the gauge assembly.
  1. Connect the C15 terminal to the positive probe of a voltmeter, and connect the negative probe to body ground. To prevent damage to the gauge assembly, lay the gauge assembly on a shop rag.
  1. Put the vehicle in neutral with the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Slowly rotate (over 3 km/h (2 mph)) one wheel with the other wheel blocked.
Does voltage pulse from 0 to about 5 V or more?
YES -
Replace the gauge assembly.■
NO -
Turn the ignition switch OFF, and go to Step 6 .
  1. Connect the No. 9 terminal of the PCM 31P connector E to the positive probe of a voltmeter, and connect the negative probe to body ground.
  1. Put the vehicle in neutral with the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Slowly rotate (over 3 km/h (2 mph)) on wheel with the other wheel blocked.
Does voltage pulse from 0 to about 5 V or more?
YES -
Repair open in the BLU/YEL wire between the gauge assembly and the PCM .■
NO -
Turn the ignition switch OFF, and go to Step 9 .
  1. Disconnect the 31P connector E from the PCM.
  1. Check for continuity between No. 25 terminal and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to ground in the BLU/YEL wire between the PCM and the gauge assembly.■
NO -
Check for these problems:
  • Bent, loose or corroded terminals.
  • A faulty PCM .■
