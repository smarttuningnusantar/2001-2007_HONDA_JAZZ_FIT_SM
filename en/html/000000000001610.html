DTC Troubleshooting: 46, 47
DTC 46, 47:
 Motor Relay Stuck ON or Stuck OFF
  1. Clear the DTC.
  1. Start the engine.
Does the EPS indicator come on?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
The system is OK at this time.■
  1. Stop the engine, and verify the DTC.
Is DTC 46 or 47 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Perform the appropriate troubleshooting for the code indicated.■
  1. Make sure the ignition switch is OFF, then remove the motor relay and test it.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
With the motor relay removed, and go to Step 5 .
NO -
Replace the motor relay (Replace the motor relay and EPS control unit together as a set).■
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery.
  1. Disconnect the EPS control unit connector A (4P).
  1. Check for continuity between the EPS control unit connector A (4P) terminal No. 3 and the motor relay 4P connector terminal No. 3.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Repair open in the GRN wire between the EPS control unit and motor relay.■
  1. Disconnect the motor 2P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between the motor relay 4P connector terminal No. 1 and motor 2P connector terminal No. 1 (RHD: No. 2).
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 10 .
NO -
Repair open in the WHT/RED wire between the motor and motor relay.■
  1. Reconnect the negative cable to the battery.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the motor relay 4P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Repair short to power in the + circuit wire between the motor relay and motor.■
NO -
Go to Step 13 .
  1. Measure the voltage between the motor relay 4P connector terminal No. 3 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Repair short to power in the + circuit wire between the motor relay and motor.■
NO -
Go to Step 14 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check for continuity between the motor relay 4P connector terminal No. 3 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the GRN wire between the EPS control unit and motor relay.■
NO -
Go to Step 16 .
  1. Check for continuity between the motor relay 4P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the WHT/RED wire between the motor and motor relay.■
NO -
Go to Step 17 .
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Measure the voltage between the motor relay 4P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 19 .
NO -
Repair short or open in the PNK/BLU wire between the EPS control unit and motor relay.■
  1. Stop the engine, and the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the negative cable from the battery.
  1. Disconnect the EPS control unit connector B (14P).
  1. Reconnect the negative cable to the battery.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Measure the voltage between the motor relay 4P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Repair short to power in the + circuit wire between the EPS control unit and motor relay.■
NO -
Go to Step 25 .
  1. Stop the engine and the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Check for continuity between the motor relay 4P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the PNK/BLU wire between the EPS control unit and motor relay.■
NO -
Go to Step 27 .
  1. Check for continuity between the motor relay 4P connector terminal No. 4 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Check for loose EPS motor control unit connectors, motor connector and motor relay connector. If necessary, substitute a known-good EPS control unit and recheck.■
NO -
Repair open in the BLK wire between the motor relay and body ground (G451).■
