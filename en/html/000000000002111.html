DTC Troubleshooting: 68
DTC 68:
 Brake Pedal Position Switch (Logic)
  1. Check the No. 16 (10 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box, and reinstall the fuse if it is OK.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Replace the fuse, and recheck. If fuse is blown, go to Step 2 .
  1. Disconnect the VSA control unit 46P connector.
  1. Remove the No. 16 (10 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and the VSA control unit 46P connector terminal No. 6.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between the VSA modulator-control unit and under-hood fuse/relay box.■
NO -
Replace the VSA modulator-control unit. ■
  1. Disconnect the VSA control unit 46P connector.
  1. Measure the voltage between body ground and the VSA control unit 46P connector terminal No. 6 when depressed the brake pedal.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Check for loose connector terminals in the VSA control unit 46P connector and repair if necessary. If terminals are OK, replace the VSA modulator-control unit. ■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the VSA modulator-control unit and under-hood fuse/relay box or the brake pedal position switch and under-hood fuse/relay box.■
