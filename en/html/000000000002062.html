DTC Troubleshooting: P0801 (33-2)
DTC P0801:
 Problem in Inhibitor Solenoid Circuit
For KB, KE, KG, and KQ Models
NOTE:
  • Record all freeze data and review General Troubleshooting Information before you troubleshoot.
  • The illustration shows the example of connector terminal arrangement; connector terminal arrangement varies with the models.
  1. Disconnect inhibitor solenoid connector.
  1. Measure the resistance between connector terminals at the solenoid connector.
Is the resistance 17.1−21.0 Ω?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Replace the inhibitor solenoid. ■
  1. Disconnect PCM connector C (22P).
  1. Check for continuity between PCM connector terminal C6 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between PCM connector terminal C6 and inhibitor solenoid connector.■
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Connect inhibitor solenoid connector.
  1. Disconnect PCM connector A (31P).
  1. Measure the resistance between PCM connector terminals C6 and A4 or A5.
Is the resistance 17.1−21.0 Ω?
YES -
Update the PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good PCM, replace the original PCM .■
NO -
Go to Step 8 .
  1. Disconnect inhibitor solenoid connector.
  1. Check for continuity between PCM connector terminal C6 and inhibitor solenoid connector terminal No. 2.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between inhibitor solenoid connector terminal No. 1 and ground (G101), or repair poor ground (G101).■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminal C6 and inhibitor solenoid connector.■
