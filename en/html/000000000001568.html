Intake Manifold Removal and Installation

Exploded View
Removal
  1. Remove the intake manifold cover.
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing.
  1. Remove the brake booster vacuum hose (A) and positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) hose (B).
  1. Remove the harness holder (A) and water bypass hose clamp (B).
  1. Remove the evaporative emission (EVAP) canister hose.

When Replacing the Intake Manifold:
  1. Remove the breather hose from the cylinder head cover.
  1. When replacing the intake mainfold, remove the throttle body.
  1. If the engine coolant was drained, remove the water bypass hoses.
  1. Remove the engine wire harness connectors and wire harness clamps from the intake manifold.
    • Throttle actuator connector
    • Manifold absolute pressure (MAP) sensor connector
    • Evaporative emission (EVAP) canister purge valve connector
  1. Remove the intake manifold bracket A (A), then remove the intake manifold (B) and exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) plate (C).
Installation
  1. Install the exhaust gas recirculation (EGR) plate (A) with a new gasket (B).
  1. Install the intake manifold (C) and tighten the bolts/nuts in a crisscross pattern in two or three steps, beginning with the inner bolt. Using the new O-rings (D).
  1. Loosely install the intake manifold bracket A (E), and tighten the bolt (F) first, then the bolt (G).
  1. Install the water bypass hoses, if it was removed.
  1. Install the throttle body (A) with a new O-ring (B), if it was removed.
  1. Install the breather hose to the cylinder head cover.
  1. Install the evaporative emission (EVAP) canister hose.

If Intake Manifold was Replaced:
  1. Install the harness holder (A) and water bypass hose clamp (B).
  1. Install the brake booster vacuum hose (A) and positive crankcase ventilation (PCV) hose (B).
  1. Install the air cleaner housing.
  1. Install the intake manifold cover.
  1. After installation, check that all tubes, hoses and connectors are installed correctly.
  1. If engine coolant drained, refill the radiator with engine coolant, and bleed air from the cooling system with the heater valve open.
