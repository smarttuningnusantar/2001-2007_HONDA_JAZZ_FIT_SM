How to Troubleshoot CVT Systems
How to Check for DTCs with the PGM Tester/Scan Tool
When the PCM senses an abnormality in the input or output systems, the [D] indicator (A) in the gauge assembly (B) will usually blink and/or the malfunction indicator lamp (MIL) will come on.
The illustration shows 6-position CVT +7 Speed mode; 6-position CVT is similar.
When the 16P data link connector (DLC) (A) (located under the dash on driver's side) is connected to the OBD Scan Tool or Honda Diagnostic System (HDS) as shown, it will indicate the diagnostic trouble code (DTC).
NOTE: The illustration shows RHD model; LHD model is symmetrical.
If the [D] indicator or MIL has been reported on, or if a driveability problem is suspected, follow this procedure:
  1. Connect the OBD Scan Tool or Honda PGM Tester to the DLC. (See the OBD Scan Tool or HDS user's manual for specific instructions. If you are using the HDS, make sure it is set to the SAE DTC type).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II), and observe the DTC on the screen.
  1. Record all fuel and emissions DTCs, A/T DTCs, and freeze data.
  1. If there is a fuel and emissions DTC, first check the fuel and emissions system as indicated by the DTC except for DTC P0700. DTC P0700 means there is one or more A/T DTC, and no problems were detected in the fuel and emissions circuit of the PCM.
  1. Reset the DTC stored in the PCM with the OBD Scan Tool or HDS.
  1. Calibrate the feedback signal for the start clutch control.
  1. Drive the vehicle for several minutes in the freeze data conditions, or at speeds over 50 km/h (30 mph), and then recheck for DTC(s). If the A/T DTC returns, go to the DTC Troubleshooting Index. If the DTC does not return, there was an intermittent problem within the circuit. Make sure all pins and terminals in the circuit are tight.
  1. Reset the radio preset stations, and set the clock.
    NOTE: You can also check for DTCs with SCS signal terminals short-circuited using with the special tool (DLC pin box 07WAJ-0010100).
How to Troubleshoot Circuits at the PCM
  1. Remove the glove box cover and glove box.
  1. Remove the passenger's lower cover, then remove the access cover.
  1. Inspect the circuit on the PCM, according to the DTC troubleshooting, with a digital multitester (A) and tapered tip probe (B) as shown.
  1. If you cannot get to the wire side of the connector or the wire side is sealed, disconnect the connector and use the tester probe to probe the connectors from the terminal side. Do not force the probe into the connector.
PCM Reset Procedure
  1. Write down the radio station presets.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Remove the No. 20 ECU fuse (15A) (A) from the under-hood fuse/relay box (B) for more than 10 seconds.
How to End a Troubleshooting Session
This procedure must be done after any troubleshooting.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the HDS from the DLC (A).
    NOTE: The illustration shows RHD model; LHD model is symmetrical.
  1. Reset the PCM.
  1. Calibrate the feedback signal for the start clutch control.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. To verify that the problem is repaired, test-drive the vehicle for several minutes at speed over 50 km/h (30 mph).

Use this procedure when you have to substitute a known-good PCM in a troubleshooting procedure.
Update the PCM only if the PCM does not already have the latest software loaded.
Do not turn the ignition switch OFF while up datating the PCM. If you turn the ignition switch OFF, the PCM can be damaged.
How to Update the PCM
NOTE:
  • To ensure the latest program is installed, update a PCM whenever the PCM is substituted or replaced.
  • You cannot update a PCM with the program it already has. It will only accept a new program.
  • Before you update the PCM, make sure the vehicle's battery is fully charged.
  • To prevent PCM damaged, do not operate any electrical system; audio system, brake, air conditioning, power windows, moonroof, and door locks, during the update.
  • If you need to diagnose the Honda interface module (HIM) becase the HIM's red (#3) light came on or was flashing during the update, leave the ignition switch in the ON (II) position when you disconnect the HIM from the DLC. This will prevent PCM damage.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON(II). Do not start the engine.
  1. Connect the Honda interface modure (HIM) to the DLC (A) located under the dash on the driver's side.
    NOTE: The illustration shows RHD model; LHD model is symmetrical.
  1. Update the PCM according to the procedures described on the HIM label. If the software in the PCM is the latest, replace the PCM.
How to Substitute the PCM
  1. Remove the passenger's dashboard under cover, side kick panel, and access cover.
  1. Disconnect PCM connectors, and remove the bolts securing the PCM.
  1. Remove the PCM, and install a known-good PCM.
  1. Rewrite the immobilizer code with the PCM replacement procedure in the HDS; this will allow you to start the engine.
  1. After completing your test, reinstall the original PCM and rewrite the immobilizer code with the PCM replacement procedure in the HDS again.
